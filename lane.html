What is Lane?

In Lanes are the main paths that minions follow toward the Nexus.
Due to this, they are defended by turrets planted along the path that must be destroyed in order to push into the enemy base.
Each successive turret in a lane must be destroyed before damage can be dealt to the next structure.
Along each lane are located areas of brush for tactical maneuvering, such as juking and ganking.
Controlling a lane is a key tactical component of gameplay; early lane control in a game can put your opponents at a disadvantage by keeping them away from your turrets and denying last hits on minions for much-needed Gold.

Top

A side lane is hard to escape from- and with only one champion to man the advance,
durable fighters and tanks are the most suited to survive on their own.

Middle

A Mage Mage or Slayer Assassin is focused on gathering experience to gain access to their abilities,
which the middle lane can effect other lanes because it is close to top and bottom lane.
The lane's smaller space is also attractive for champions with bad defense to easily retreat to safety under turret.

bottom

A Ad Carry is focused on obtaining items rather than gaining their abilities,
so they won't mind a companion sharing experience.

jungle

Junglers rely on killing neutral monsters in the jungle to keep up with their laning teammates in terms of gold and experience.
Junglers go to other lanes to help the laners to kill the other.
